CAMELS : Interesting Facts

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

CHAMALEONS : Interesting Facts

  • Chamaleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder .
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chamaleons .
  • Most chamaleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches .

PIGEONS : Interesting Facts
  • Pigeons can move their wings ten times per second and maintain heartbeats at the rate of 600 times per minute, during the period of 16 hours.
  • Pigeons can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing capabilities .

TEDDY BEARS : Interesting Facts
  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall!

PANDAS : Interesting Facts
  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

FLYING CICADAS : Interesting Facts
  • Cicadas are like tiny violins .
  • Cicadas make noise like toy frogs .
  • Their short adult life is not unusual for bugs.

FROGS : Intersting Facts
  • Frogs absorb water through their skin so they don't need to drink.
  • Frogs can lay as many as 4,000 eggs in frogspawn.
  • The eyes and nose of a frog are on top of its head so it can breathe and see when most of its body is under the water.

DOGS : Interesting Facts
  • Dogs noses are wet to help absorb scent chemicals.
  • Three dogs survived the Titanic sinking.
  • Their sense of smell is at least 40x better than ours.
